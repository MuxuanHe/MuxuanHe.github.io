About Me

I am a first year grad student in linguistics at the University of Southern California. I identify myself as a pyscholinguist, but I also have interests in syntax and sociolinguistics. Recently, I found the apical vowels in my dialect (Wuxue dialect) are very interesting but remain understudied, which will become my future phonetics project. I work on projects that intrigue me and try my best not to get overwhelmed by my curiosity.

My current research projects include the processing of negation (psycholinguistics), Chinese plural marker -men (sociolinguistics), you in mei(you) (syntax).

Apart from linguistics, I also love photography, music and travelling.
